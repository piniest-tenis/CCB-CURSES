*The Grandmarshal's audience chamber is a long, cold room at the heart of Eskikale Keep. Grandmarshal Pogg sits at the far end upon a chair that is not quite a throne, attended by two manservants who never leave his side. The Grandmarshal speaks, but his words come as though from a great distance — fragmented, circular, addressing people and events that may or may not exist. The manservants translate, interpret, and occasionally answer for him entirely. An elaborate system of protocol has calcified around these interactions: visitors must address the Grandmarshal through the manservants, must not look directly at the Grandmarshal while he is "composing his thoughts," must present gifts in descending order of weight, and must not speak the word "forest" within the chamber. No one remembers who established these rules. Everyone follows them.*
**Tone**: Surreal, oppressive, darkly absurd
**Tier 2**
**Type**: Social
**Difficulty**: 14

**Potential Adversaries**:
- [[Frightened Villagers]]

**Features**
**The Protocol - Passive**
PCs who violate one of the court's arcane rules (speaking out of turn, addressing the Grandmarshal directly, using forbidden words, presenting gifts incorrectly) must immediately make a Presence Reaction Roll (14). On a failure, the manservants declare the audience suspended and the PCs are escorted out. On a success, the manservants allow a single correction. A second violation in the same audience ends it regardless.
_"His Excellency is composing his thoughts. You will wait. You will not look at him while he composes. This is the third rule."_

**The Manservants - Passive**
The two manservants — quiet, efficient, terrified — control all access to the Grandmarshal. Any attempt to bypass them or speak to Pogg directly requires a Presence Roll (15). On a success, Pogg responds with a moment of startling lucidity before retreating again. On a failure, the manservants intervene physically, and the PC marks a Stress.
_"The Grandmarshal has heard your petition and finds it... He says the lake is singing again. He means he will consider it. Please, just — let us handle this."_

**Pogg's Lucidity - Active**
Spend a Fear. The Grandmarshal has a moment of terrible clarity. He looks directly at a PC and speaks coherently about something he should not know — the state of the Creep, a secret one of the Rearcaptains is keeping, or the true nature of something the party has encountered. The moment passes, and the manservants rush to redirect the conversation, visibly shaken.
_"He looked at me. Right at me. And he said, 'It's already inside the walls.' Then he asked his manservant for more tea."_

**Exposure - Reaction**
When a PC succeeds on a Knowledge or Instinct Roll (13) while in the audience chamber, they notice signs of the Grandmarshal's Creep exposure — the faint dark lines beneath his skin, the way his fingers occasionally move independent of his will, the moments when his eyes focus on something no one else can see. This information is valuable but dangerous; the manservants notice the PC noticing and become Hostile toward them for the remainder of the scene.
_"You see it now. The way his hand twitches. The pattern in the veins at his temple. It's not age. It's not illness. You've seen this before, at the edge of the treeline."_
