*A sprawling, semi-permanent market crowded along the outer walls of the Eskikale Keep gatehouse. Traders from Reveille hawk Carnithian goods alongside local vendors peddling lake fish, Teetle jerky, and Paarlid carvings. Beneath the legitimate commerce, a somewhat-tolerated black market operates in plain sight — Lanmaoa extracts, unregistered Sallidean weapons, and stranger things whispered about in the gaps between stalls. The Hygiane Order tolerates it because the market is the only thing keeping Gatehouse fed.*
**Tone**: Bustling, opportunistic, underhanded
**Tier 1**
**Type**: Social
**Difficulty**: 11

**Potential Adversaries**:
- [[Frightened Villagers]]
- [[Lanmaoa]]

**Features**
**Open Trade - Passive**
PCs can purchase common supplies and equipment at standard prices. Unique or rare items require a Presence Roll (11) to locate a willing seller. On a failure, the item is available but at double cost.
_"Everything you need is here, friend. Everything you want costs extra."_

**Black Market - Active**
A PC seeking illicit, rare, or restricted goods must make a Presence Roll (13). On a success, they find what they need but attract the attention of one of the market's power brokers, who will expect a favor in return. On a failure, they are noticed by a member of the Hygiane Order and must explain themselves.
_"You didn't get that from me. In fact, you've never seen me. In fact, I was never here. Are we clear?"_

**Pickpocket's Paradise - Reaction**
When a PC rolls with Fear while in the Market, a thief lifts a small item from them — a coinpurse, a trinket, a letter. The PC may make an Instinct Roll (12) to notice the theft immediately. Otherwise, they discover the loss at a dramatically inconvenient moment.
_"The crowd presses in, and for a moment you feel a hand brush past you. Just the crowd. Probably."_

**Rumor Mill - Active**
Spend a Fear. A merchant or local shares a rumor — partially true, partially embellished. The rumor concerns one of: the Grandmarshal's declining health, the Etherements' experiments, strange behavior in the lake, or the growing boldness of Forestdown's edge. The PC must make a Knowledge Roll (11) to separate truth from embellishment.
_"My cousin's husband's sister works the north tower laundry. She says the Etherements haven't sent out their linens in three weeks. Three weeks! What are they doing in there?"_
