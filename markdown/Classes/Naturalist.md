### Class Items

_A piece of hide or hair from an animal_ or _a piece of carved wood or bone_

### Hope Feature

_Natural Balance_: Spend 3 Hope and until the end of the scene you may use your Presence and Instinct in place of one another until the end of the scene.

### Class Feature

**Keen Senses**
Spend a Hope when you make an Instinct roll to investigate. On a success, learn the normal details you otherwise would, plus gain one of the following details or benefits:

- How recently [[Character|Character(s)]] or [[Creature|Creature(s)]] last touched or interacted with the object of your investigation
- General details about the species of those character(s) or creature(s) (e.g. They were aquatic, mammals, arthropods, etc)
- A +1 bonus to the [[Attitude]] of characters witnessing you investigate

# Subclasses

## **Feral Wonder**

_Play the Feral Wonder if you want to lean into your creature aspects and be a deadly and efficient operator in the natural world._

### Spellcast Trait

Instinct

### Foundation Features

_Natural Weapons_: When you make an unarmed attack, treat it as if you had a corresponding weapon of your current Tier. You may mark a Stress to make a second attack immediately after your first.
_Survivalist_: During a rest, roll a number of d6 using your Proficiency, and store the results. You can add the results to any Instinct roll made by you or an ally. Clear unused dice at the start of your next rest.

### Specialization Feature

**At Home In The Wild**: You become unremarkable to all but the most territorial [[Creature|Creatures]] in Tidwell. Once per rest, you can activate this feature. When you do, for the rest of the scene most creatures will ignore you unless you interact with them directly.

### Mastery Feature

**Apex Form**: Once per scene, spend 1 Hope to gain Advantage on all unarmed attacks until the end of your next turn. During this time, you also ignore difficult terrain.

## **Practiced Observer**

_Play the Practiced Observer if you want to use your animalistic traits to bolster your impressive influential abilities._

### Spellcast Trait

Instinct

### Foundation Features

_Sniff it Out_: After you use _Keen Senses_, you can spend a Hope to determine the direction characters or creatures identified via this manner have gone. When you arrive at the destination, you can use your Class Feature again without spending a Hope.
_Jumpy_: Unless a creature with the _Hidden_ condition attacks you or your party first, you can choose to start combat with the spotlight. If you do so, you can move up to Far range this turn without making a roll.

### Specialization Feature

**Strive to Stay Alive**: When you fail an Instinct roll with Fear, you can mark 2 Stress to add your Agility, Finesse or Strength score to the total of your Hope die. If this changes the result to a failure with Hope, you gain a Hope as you would normally. If this changes the result to a success with Fear, clear a Stress. If it changes the result to a success with Hope, treat it as a Critical Success.

### Mastery Feature

**Perfect Track**: After using _Sniff it Out_, you can spend 1 Hope to ask the GM one direct question about the tracked target's immediate plans. The answer must be truthful and useful.

## Mechanical Notes

Keen Senses rewards curiosity: spend Hope on Instinct investigation rolls to gain tactical details, species information, or a social edge simultaneously. The Feral Wonder path is strongest in natural environments where unarmed attacks and _Survivalist_ dice can swing fights; distribute your stored dice to allies at critical moments rather than holding them until they expire. The Practiced Observer path builds directly on your class feature: use _Sniff it Out_ to refresh Keen Senses for free, keeping your investigative momentum running through multi-scene situations. Both paths benefit from treating Instinct investigations as a core part of your action economy, not as side activities.
