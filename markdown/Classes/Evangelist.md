### Class Items

_A worn stole in the colors of a faith that isn't your own_ or _a small carved idol of something that may be listening_

### Hope Feature

_Divine Misdirection_: Spend 3 Hope. Until the end of the scene, every time you successfully deceive a target using Sacred Deception, you also cause them to clear 1 Stress. They feel briefly at peace — uplifted, even — about what just happened to them.

### Class Feature

**Sacred Deception**
Once per scene, you may frame a lie, misdirection, or manipulative act as a devotional offering to a power you claim to serve. When you do, the deception is bolstered by apparent sincerity: gain Advantage on any roll made as part of this deception, and anyone who would normally be suspicious of you gains a temporary +1 bonus to their [[Attitude]] towards you for the duration of the scene.

### Subclasses

As an Evangelist, choose between the following subclasses.

**Revival Circuit**
_Play the Revival Circuit if you want to work crowds, collect devotion like dues, and leave town before the faithful realize the miracle didn't take._

**Fool God's Envoy**
_Play the Fool God's Envoy if you want your con to carry an unsettling ring of prophecy — the kind of trickster that makes people wonder if something out there is actually using you._

### Background Questions

_Answer any of the following background questions. You can also create your own questions._

- What congregation, order, or flock did you first deceive — and do any of them still believe?
- A mark once showed you genuine faith in something, and it made you feel a way you didn't expect. What happened next?
- Whose divine authority do you invoke most often, and have you ever worried something might actually hear you?

### Connections

_Ask your fellow players one of the following questions for their characters to answer, or create your own questions._

- I performed a miracle for you once. Only one of us knows it wasn't real. Which one is it?
- You've watched me preach to a crowd and caught me believing it for a moment. Did you say anything?
- I promised you divine protection as part of a con. Something went right anyway. What do you think happened?

# Subclasses

## **Revival Circuit**

_Play the Revival Circuit if you want to work crowds, collect devotion like dues, and leave town before the faithful realize the miracle didn't take._

### Spellcast Trait

Presence

### Foundation Features

_Pass the Burden_: When you use Sacred Deception in front of a crowd or group, its effects extend beyond your primary target. All witnesses within Very Close range have gain a temporary +1 bonus to their [[Attitude]] towards you for the rest of the scene.

_Altar Call_: Once per scene, after a successful Presence roll in a social context, treat the target as having granted you a [[Favor]]. You may call in this Favor at any point this session to make one reasonable request of that character; at the GM's discretion, the request is fulfilled. If the request is denied, you retain the Favor.

### Specialization Feature

**The Testimony**: Once per scene, publicly deliver a false account of your own divine encounter or personal transformation. Make a Presence roll against each target within Far range. On a success, each target has their Attitude toward you improve by 1 and is disinclined to question your motives for the rest of the scene. On a success with Hope, one character of your choice is also compelled to corroborate your account if asked.

### Mastery Feature

**Tent Revival**: Once per session, hold court as a preacher or prophet over a scene. Make a Presence roll against a Difficulty set by the GM based on the crowd's disposition. On a success, every character present will take one significant action you direct, believing it to be either their own conviction or a genuine divine instruction, provided that action does not require self-harm. On a success with Hope, you may also name one character in the crowd who is moved to become a persistent sympathizer for the rest of the session.

## **The Fool God's Envoy**

_Play the Fool God's Envoy if you want your con to carry an unsettling ring of prophecy — the kind of trickster that makes people wonder if something out there is actually using you._

### Spellcast Trait

Instinct

### Foundation Features

_Hallowed Mischief_: Once per rest, when you activate an effect from a Trickery domain card, describe a small, strange unintended side effect — something that wasn't part of your plan. The GM incorporates it into the scene. It may be inconvenient, useful, or simply inexplicable. If you do so, clear a Stress.

_Divine Confusion_: Once per scene, cause a Character to become confused about their own intentions. They must spend their next action reconsidering what they're doing rather than acting.

### Specialization Feature

**Oracle of the Absurd**: Once per scene, deliver a prophecy or divine proclamation to a target. Make an Instinct roll. On a success, name one specific thing that will occur before the end of the scene; the GM incorporates it as true. On a failure, the proclamation unsettles the target anyway — they mark 1 Stress and are distracted for the duration of the scene.

### Mastery Feature

**The Actual Miracle**: Once per session, something you faked or performed as misdirection turns out to be real. Describe the false miracle; the GM tells you what actually happened. It may be more, less, or differently wondrous than you intended, but it is genuine.

## Mechanical Notes

Use Sacred Deception early in a scene as the Attitude bonus shapes the whole interaction that follows, not just a single roll. The Revival Circuit is built around Presence rolls and crowds: Pass the Burden extends your deception's goodwill to everyone watching, so prioritize using Sacred Deception when there's an audience. Bank Favors from Altar Call rather than spending them immediately; a Favor held until a critical moment is worth far more than a quick ask. The Testimony is your mid-scene escalation. With it, a single Presence roll that can shift the Attitude of an entire room, and a success with Hope locks in a corroborator. Tent Revival is your session-defining move. If you set it up with prior deceptions so the crowd is already warmed up, you can see some extremely powerful results from its use. The Fool God's Envoy rewards leaning into the chaos of your own abilities: trigger Hallowed Mischief on every Trickery card activation to accumulate Stress clears, and let the GM's side effects complicate the fiction in ways you can exploit. Oracle of the Absurd is most powerful when you make a bold, specific claim and a successful Instinct roll lets you author one true fact about the scene, so name something tactically useful. The Actual Miracle is strongest when the false miracle you performed was something genuinely ambitious.
