### Class Items

_A remnant of your former faith kept for reasons you haven't fully examined_ or _something that appeared the day you broke your vow and hasn't left you since_

### Hope Feature

_The Price Made Power_: Spend 3 Hope. Your next use of Broken Vow grants both its damage effect and one additional effect of your choice.

### Class Feature

**Broken Vow**
Once per scene, invoke one of your broken oaths as supernatural fuel. Describe the vow you violated: a promise to a deity, a sacred commitment abandoned, a religious duty betrayed. Choose one effect:

- Deal d8 magic damage to a target within Close range, using your Proficiency.
- Create a supernatural disturbance in the immediate area, treating this as the effect of a Level 1 Weird domain card of your choice.
- Gain a +2 bonus to your next Spellcast Roll this scene.

### Subclasses

As an Oathbreaker Mystic, choose between the following subclasses.

**Apostate**
_Play the Apostate if you want to wield the strange forces that haunt you after your broken oath._

**Branded Penitent**
_Play the Branded Penitent if you want the tension between what you owe and what you've taken to be the engine that drives you._

### Background Questions

_Answer any of the following background questions. You can also create your own questions._

- What did you swear, and to whom? Do you remember the moment you broke it?
- What appeared to you on the other side of that breaking? Did it speak?
- Is there anything you would refuse even now, or have you accepted you are past that point?

### Connections

_Ask your fellow players one of the following questions for their characters to answer, or create your own questions._

- You witnessed me doing something I had sworn I would never do. Did you try to stop me?
- I've told you about the thing that answered when I broke my vow. Do you believe me?
- There's a faith practice I still carry, even now. You've noticed it. What is it?

# Subclasses

## **Apostate**

_Play the Apostate if you want to wield the strange forces that haunt you after your broken oath._

### Spellcast Trait

Knowledge

### Foundation Features

_Open Vessel_: When you use Broken Vow, you may also manifest a minor supernatural phenomenon in the area: doors close sharply, lights bend, a voice half-heard. [[Character|Characters]] who witness this and are unaccustomed to the supernatural mark a Stress.

_Alien Inheritance_: Once per rest, when you take damage from a supernatural or magical source, spend 1 Hope to absorb its nature. Until the end of the scene, your attacks deal an additional 2d6 damage and count as that type damage in addition to your usual type.

### Specialization Feature

**Unsacred Ground**: Once per scene, spend 2 Hope. The space within Very Close range of you becomes hostile to all characters except you. They must mark a Stress at the start of each of their turns while they remain in that space. This lasts until the end of the scene or you dismiss it.

### Mastery Feature

**What Answered Back**: Once per session, the power you traded your faith for grants you a favor. Choose one spell-like effect from any Weird domain card of Level 3 or lower and apply it now without requiring a Spellcast Roll.

## **Branded Penitent**

_Play the Branded Penitent if you want the tension between what you owe and what you've taken to be the engine that drives you._

### Spellcast Trait

Presence

### Foundation Features

_Penitent's Endurance_: When you use Broken Vow, heal 1 Hit Point in addition to the chosen effect. The divine wound feeds you even as it punishes you.

_Sacred Scar_: Once per rest, when you take damage, mark a Stress instead of a Hit Point. When you do, add a token to this card. At the end of the scene, spend these tokens to add +2 per token to your next Spellcast Roll.

### Specialization Feature

**Still Bound**: Once per scene, invoke the name of the deity whose vow you broke. All characters with any religious conviction must mark 1 Stress or grant you the right to speak without interruption through your next action. Adversaries with religious convictions make their next attack against you with Disadvantage.

### Mastery Feature

**The Debt Remembered**: Once per session, the divine power you betrayed intervenes, not in forgiveness but in use. Choose one: the GM sends you a vision with tactical or narrative significance, or a divine protective effect activates for your allies for the rest of the scene (treat as Aura of Mercy at full strength), whether you want it to or not.

## Mechanical Notes

Your class feature is tied directly to your backstory. Work with your GM to establish a list of the specific vows you broke and keep it accessible during play. The more precise the vow you invoke, the more resonant the fiction. The Apostate path plays aggressively with supernatural effects and builds toward a growing arsenal of alien power; the Penitent path builds resilience through sacrifice and draws mechanical strength from the ongoing relationship with a power that still has claim on you.
