### Class Items

_A clutch of opalescent feathers_ or _a jug of bitter-tasting green liquid_

### Hope Feature

_Recall_: Spend 3 Hope to call a dissipated _Ghostly Companion_ back to service.

### Class Features

**Ghostly Companion**
You gain a special downtime project called _Attract Companion_ with a Countdown Tracker of 3. When you complete this project, you gain a unique item called a _Wraithcaller's Lure_. You can use this item to attempt to befriend an ethereal creature you encounter in Tidwell. This creature follows the rules provided for [[Ranger Companions]].

**Veilstep**
Once per scene, spend 1 Hope to move up to Very Close range through creatures and objects as a flicker of spectral motion. This movement cannot end inside solid matter.

### Subclasses

As a Wraithcaller, choose between the following subclasses.

**Grovetender**
_Play the Grovetender if you want to command companion spirits and hold the line between the real and ethereal._

**Mire Whisperer**
_Play the Mire Whisperer if you want to manipulate fear, portents and luck via supernatural abilities._

# Subclasses

## **Grovetender**

_Play the Grovetender if you want to command companion spirits and hold the line between life and death._

### Spellcast Trait

Instinct

### Foundation Features

_Silent Procession_: Once per scene, you may direct your Ghostly Companion to appear anywhere within Close range. This happens instantaneously and without notice by adversaries.
_Pale Shelter_: Once per scene, when an ally within Close range receives damage, spend 1 Hope to reduce that damage by your Spellcast Trait.

### Specialization Feature

**Unquiet Chorus**: Mark 1 Stress to _Empower_ your companion until end of Scene. While empowered, it gains Advantage on one attack roll. While _Empowered_, your companion can impose _Hidden_ on you or an ally within Very Close range once.

### Mastery Feature

**Sovereign of Echoes**: Once per session, when your companion dissipates via accumulated stress, it immediately reforms with no Stress marked. When this happens, you gain 1 Hope.

## **Mire Whisperer**

_Play the Mire Whisperer if you want to manipulate fear, omen, and rumor through eerie social pressure._

### Spellcast Trait

Presence

### Foundation Features

_Whisper Through Bone_: Once per scene, make a Spellcast Roll against a [[Character]] you can see. On a success, ask one direct question they must answer honestly or mark 1 Stress.
_Bad Omen_: Once per scene, when you succeed with Fear on a roll, choose a [[Character]] within Far range. That character takes a -1 penalty to their next roll before the end of the scene.

### Specialization Feature

**Hex of Unrest**: Once per scene, make a Spellcast Roll against a [[Character]] within Far range. On a success, they take a -2 penalty to their next roll. If they are already affected by _Bad Omen_, they also mark 1 Stress.

### Mastery Feature

**Name in the Dark**: Once per scene, when a target fails while affected by _Bad Omen_ or **Hex of Unrest**, you can either gain 1 Hope or clear 1 Stress.

## Mechanical Notes

Your two class features work together: use Veilstep to reposition before key actions, then apply your subclass features from the resulting position. Both are Hope-free or Hope-light, so conserve Hope for subclass abilities. The Grovetender path centers on your Ghostly Companion's placement. Silent Procession lets you reposition it without telegraphing; use it before combat reaches your allies rather than reactively. Sovereign of Echoes is most valuable when your companion has been under sustained pressure. Let it work hard, then bring it back if it falls. The Mire Whisperer path rewards patience: apply Bad Omen first, then stack Hex of Unrest, and let Name in the Dark pay out when targets fail. Don't spend the condition early before it has a chance to trigger.
