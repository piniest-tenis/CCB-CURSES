### Class Feature
**Impressive Recall**
Your ability to remember details about the people you meet has endeared you to many. Once per rest, you can spend a Hope to learn an inconsequential detail about a [[Character]] with whom you are interacting. When you drop this detail in your conversation with this [[Character]], they will treat you with a +1 bonus to [[Attitude]] until the end of the scene.

### Background Questions
_Answer any of the following background questions. You can also create your own questions._
- At some point I've chosen books over people. Do I regret this, and do I choose differently now?
- What discoveries have I made as part of my studies, and have I gained any notoriety from them?
- Do I seek out others to form deep connections, or do I use my social skills to keep people satisfied with less than my true self?
### Connections
_Ask your fellow players one of the following questions for their characters to answer, or create your own questions._
- I once woke you up from rest to excitedly tell you about something I'd discovered. What was it, and how did you respond?
- What was our first meeting like? Where did it happen, and how did you feel about me?
- I've experimented on you before. How did you discover it, and what was your reaction?
# Subclasses
## **Socialite**
_Play the Socialite if you want to use your impressive knowledge to bolster your natural charm._

### Spellcast Trait

Presence

### Foundation Features

_Mover and Shaker_: When you first meet a new [[Faction]], you can spend a Hope to gain advantage on any roll to influence its [[Adherent|Adherents]].
_Calculated Pressure_: When you _Prepare_ as a downtime move, you can choose a character that you know to give the _Mark_ condition. When you attempt to influence a character with the _Mark_ condition, treat a success with Hope as a _Critical Success_. The _Mark_ condition ends after your first roll to influence that character.

### Specialization Feature

**Cruel Words**: Once per scene, before you deal damage to a [[Character]] you can issue a scathing remark to your target and deal an additional d8 damage using your Proficiency.

### Mastery Feature

**Networked Intellect**: Once per session, after you successfully influence a [[Faction]] [[Adherent]], choose one ally. That ally gains Advantage on their next roll against the same faction this session.
## **Bookworm**
_Play the Bookworm if you want to gain powerful benefits from your knowledge of the physical world._

### Spellcast Trait

Knowledge

### Foundation Features

_Eidetic Memory_: Spend a Hope to gain Advantage on your next Knowledge Roll.
_Deep Research_: When you _Prepare_ as a downtime move, you can clear 1 Stress in addition to the normal benefits.

### Specialization Feature

**Studied Threat**: Spend a Hope to add your Knowledge score to your Evasion until the end of the scene.

### Mastery Feature

**Grand Thesis**: Once per scene, after a successful Knowledge roll, you can spend 1 Hope to either clear 1 Stress for yourself or grant an ally within Far range a +2 bonus to their next roll.

## Mechanical Notes

Impressive Recall rewards social preparation, so use it at the start of conversations rather than mid-scene. That way, the Attitude bonus shapes the whole interaction. The Socialite path rewards consistent factional work. Mover and Shaker applies every time you meet a new group, so treat each faction as an opportunity rather than a complication. Calculated Pressure is most valuable on high-stakes influence targets. Apply the Mark before your most important roll, not on easy targets. The Bookworm path trades social momentum for defensive and informational leverage. Use Studied Threat proactively when a scene shifts from dialogue to violence, so your Evasion is already reinforced before the first attack lands.
