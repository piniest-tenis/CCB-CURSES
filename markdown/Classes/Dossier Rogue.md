### Class Items

_A dossier on a target you haven't moved on yet_ or _a set of notes and tools organized with a professionalism that makes it unclear which field they belong to_

### Hope Feature

_Perfect Information_: Spend 3 Hope. You may choose all four options from Advanced Reconnaissance simultaneously.

### Class Feature

**Advanced Reconnaissance**
Once per scene, spend a moment analyzing a target, space, or object (no action during downtime; one action in combat). Choose two of the following:

- Learn one security measure protecting the target or space.
- Learn one item or piece of information the target possesses.
- Gain +2 to all Thievery rolls against this target or within this space for the rest of the scene.
- Identify one character in the scene who may be watching or acting against you.

### Subclasses

As a Dossier Rogue, choose between the following subclasses.

**Profile Artist**
_Play the Profile Artist if you want information to be your primary weapon, where the gap between what you know and what your target knows is the real advantage._

**Ghost Operative**
_Play the Ghost Operative if you want reconnaissance to mean the job is already done before you walk in._

### Background Questions

_Answer any of the following background questions. You can also create your own questions._

- What was the most detailed file you've ever compiled on someone? Did they ever know?
- What did you take that you later discovered you'd entirely misunderstood the value of?
- What made you combine your research skills with this particular line of work?

### Connections

_Ask your fellow players one of the following questions for their characters to answer, or create your own questions._

- I compiled information on you before we met. Do you know what I found?
- You've returned something I took after learning more about what it meant. Have you forgiven me for taking it?
- I've produced information that completely changed your plans. How do you feel about being surprised by me?

# Subclasses

## **Profile Artist**

_Play the Profile Artist if you want information to be your primary weapon, where the gap between what you know and what your target knows is the real advantage._

### Spellcast Trait

Knowledge

### Foundation Features

_Full Dossier_: Once per session, for a target you have previously used Advanced Reconnaissance on, ask the GM a yes/no questions about that target. Answers must be truthful.

_Pattern Recognition_: When you succeed on a roll using information gained from Advanced Reconnaissance, your next roll against that same target gains +2. This bonus refreshes each time you succeed against the same target.

### Specialization Feature

**Vulnerability Assessment**: Once per scene, when you have used Advanced Reconnaissance this scene, designate one target as fully assessed. All attack rolls against that target by you or an ally add +2 to the damage roll.

### Mastery Feature

**The Complete Picture**: Once per session, use a short rest downtime move called _Documentation_ to ask the GM three true/false questions about any target, faction, or location you've visited this session. The GM must answer all three truthfully.

## **The Ghost Operative**

_Play the Ghost Operative if you want reconnaissance to mean the job is already done before you walk in._

### Spellcast Trait

Instinct

### Foundation Features

_Informed Entry_: When you use Advanced Reconnaissance to learn a security measure, you automatically bypass that specific measure once this scene without a roll.

_Pre-Planned Exit_: Once per rest, before you enter a space, declare a specific exit route. When you take that exit, any successful roll to remain undetected grants you the _Hidden_ condition.

### Specialization Feature

**Clean Extraction**: Once per scene, after successfully acquiring an object or piece of information via thievery, you may ensure the acquisition is not discovered until the start of the next scene. Work with the GM; this doesn't rewrite what happened, only delays awareness of it.

### Mastery Feature

**The Job**: Once per session, when you execute a theft with a full plan (established between you and the GM in advance), treat every roll as having been made with Advantage. The plan must include a target, a method, and an exit. Improvising after the plan begins costs this benefit.

## Mechanical Notes

Your power scales with preparation. Use Advanced Reconnaissance consistently and early, since information gathered before you act is worth far more than information discovered after. The Profile Artist path wins through accumulated intelligence over a session; the Ghost Operative path wins through irreversible, clean execution. In either case, talk to your GM during downtime about upcoming jobs. This class is most satisfying when planning is rewarded.
