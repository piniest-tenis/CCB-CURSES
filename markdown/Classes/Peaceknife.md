### Class Items

_A concealed blade or tool kept somewhere polite_ or _a weapon concealed in a mundane item_

### Hope Feature

_Double Exit_: Spend 3 Hope. Use both effects of Always an Out simultaneously this scene without requiring a roll for either.

### Class Feature

**Always an Out**
Once per scene, when a social situation breaks down or a Thievery action fails, choose one before consequences are declared:

- **Redirect**: Describe how you smoothly steer the conversation or scene away from scrutiny. You gain the _Hidden_ condition and your next roll benefits from it.
- **Talk Through It**: Describe what you say to defuse the moment. Make a Presence roll (13). On a success, the immediate negative consequence is delayed until the start of the next scene.

### Subclasses

As a Peaceknife, choose between the following subclasses.

**Silver Diplomat**
_Play the Silver Diplomat if you want to read the room perfectly and make every exit look like an arrival._

**Moonlit Agent**
_Play the Moonlit Agent if you want to move quietly through situations and take what needs taking before anyone decides to make things complicated._

### Background Questions

_Answer any of the following background questions. You can also create your own questions._

- You've walked out of a confrontation everyone expected to end badly without throwing a punch. How?
- What is the most valuable thing you've acquired in a room full of people who thought they were watching you?
- When did you last use your skills to help someone who didn't know you were helping them?

### Connections

_Ask your fellow players one of the following questions for their characters to answer, or create your own questions._

- I once played a role in front of you so convincingly you forgot it wasn't real for a while. What role was it?
- You've wondered whether my helpfulness is always genuine. What made you doubt?
- I've talked us out of a situation my actions got us into. Do you hold that against me?

# Subclasses

## **Silver Diplomat**

_Play the Silver Diplomat if you want to read the room perfectly and make every exit look like an arrival._

Presence

_Read the Room_: Once per rest, before making a social roll, spend a moment observing the target. Ask the GM one question about the target's current emotional state, what they want from this encounter, or what would change their mind. The answer must be truthful.

_Graceful Exit_: Whenever you gain the _Hidden_ condition through your class feature, you also gain Advantage on your next roll in addition to the feature's normal benefit.

**The Long Game**: Once per scene, when you succeed on a social roll, choose one character you influenced. Until the end of the session, they will take one small action in your favor without prompting. The GM determines the form it takes.

**Everyone's Confidant**: Once per session, any character you speak with alone for more than a moment will volunteer one piece of information they didn't intend to share. You select the moment this happens after it occurs; the GM tells you what they revealed.

## **The Moonlit Agent**

_Play the Moonlit Agent if you want to move quietly through situations and take what needs taking before anyone decides to make things complicated._

Finesse

_Clean Extraction_: When you use your class feature to gain the _Hidden_ condition, you may immediately make one Thievery-related action without breaking the condition, provided the target doesn't have direct sightline to you.

_Soft Landing_: Once per rest, when you make a Thievery roll with Fear, spend 1 Hope to reroll the Fear die and take the better result.

**No Trace**: Once per scene, after a successful Thievery action, you can ensure that the action leaves no evidence traceable to you this scene. The GM cannot use evidence of this specific act to advance adversary awareness.

**Without a Trace**: Once per session, when you enter a secured space through your own skill or planning, nothing you do inside that space leaves any evidence unless you choose to leave it. This effect ends when you leave the space.

Peaceknife rewards knowing in advance whether you'll need to talk or run. Read the situation before you commit to either. Use backgrounds and Connections to establish your character as someone who plans every conversation like a job. The Diplomat path plays the long social game and builds relationships that pay out later; the Moonlit Agent path prefers decisive action with clean recovery. In either case, your class feature is strongest when you use it early, before consequences have a chance to compound.
