- `Eraldama` is the dry season that opens Varjalune’s trade routes, so Taliga is busy, stocked, and crowded when the incursion hits.
- Taliga sits on `Erelbaad Lake`, whose communities include `Ayonik` and `Allonaut` partners along the shore.
- The `Hygiane Order` at this point should feel sharp and terrifyingly effective: Tenders map perimeters, Medicaments handle contamination and containment, Hospitallers kill major threats, and no one wastes motion.
- The incursion is canonically a lakeborne spread of `Etherotaxia` out of `Forestdown`, followed by rapid `Creep` conversion of flora and fauna. The Medicaments and Hospitallers save Taliga, but only after severe losses.

**Command Pool - GM resource tracking**
I will be tracking your choices via a system on my end. When all is said and done, you will have a total of command points that you will be able to allocate to one of three areas of concern: Evacuation, Containment, and Strike.

**Evacuation** will grant you benefits in removing complications for civilians, and complications caused by civilians, after you arrive in Taliga.
**Containment** will grant you benefits in avoiding or mitigating the spread of Creep and Etherotaxia, and will remove complications from its incursion into the city.
**Strike** will grant you benefits in dealing with any Ehterotaxic or Corrupted Fauna you will likely encounter as you work to eliminate the threat to Taliga.

# Part 1: Planning

The smoke from Taliga isn't the type that would signal alarm. Just a few draft fires burning lazily in chimneys, and wafting on the warm dry air of the early Eraldama season. From this distance, you would be forgiven if you believed everything was fine in Taliga. The reality is unfortunately much starker.

At the edge of the encampment south of Taliga, you are all seated on crates and stools, mercifully shaded from the overbearing heat of the midday sun. Chalk maps are drawn on dark leathers stretched between taut wooden frames. A young Medicament inductee is furiously updating a ration ledger as he tries to keep up with the Quartermaster's barked words. A group of Tenders is assembled just beyond, filling oiled sacks with salt from casks on the back of a Teetle cart, and runners have been filtering in and out of the shaded pavilion that serves as the beating heart of the Hygiane Order's forward operating command center. Most disconcertingly, a glass vessel sits on the table in front of you. Inside, a twisted heap of silvery scales and fins jut out at odd angles. A large spiny crest twists along what you can only assume is a spine, which terminates in a head devoid of features except for a slimy black maw. The creature jerks suddenly, and a glob of black ooze drops from the mouth onto the glass, which it then greedily sucks back up. It seems unperturbed by the lack of water.

Just past this abomination, the commander paces with a report in hand. Despite her posture, she is obviously staring straight through the parchment. She is an older Hokaw. The crest of feathers at her head lacks the vibrancy it once held, and the cere rimming her mouth has faded to a creamy beige, contrasting her otherwise black skin. A deep groove along her beak speaks of a once-grave-but-long-since-healed injury. She sighs, her eyes coming off the field report in front of her and heading to the shoreline.

There, the last boat on the lake is finally winched to shore and parked next to the rest. The fishermen, two soft-furred and big-eyed Ayonik and one particularly stout-shelled Allonaut debark, and are ushered quickly by a group of Medicaments to a quarantine area. The boat's hold is spilled onto the shore, and a Hospitaller issues a gruff warning before lobbing a clay jar into the pile of wriggling fish. It ignites with stunning speed, even with the moisture, and adds to the already confusingly pleasant smell of cooked fish and burnt grasses.

The Hokaw commandant, Dinty Pogg, turns to the group, and drops the report on the table.

"Information from Taliga is already becoming unreliable. We know the Creep is here; that much is evident." She motions to the glass vessel on the table. "We know that its been here long enough to start affecting the lake. We know that we need to evacuate the city."

Here, she pauses, and turns towards a chalk map. "Our time is short. As my planning council, I am asking your guidance on how best to allocate our resources. Review the reports and investigate. Meet back here by midday so that we may make a determination. Keep in mind that every second we spend being unsure is another second the Creep plants itself in Taliga."

Watcher said it was hard to make out in the midday, but believed the lights to be yellow. Concern it's a sign of encroaching Etherotaxia."
