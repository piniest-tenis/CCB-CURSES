Curses

1. Often have great injustice that causes their origin
2. Sometimes spur from beliefs about oneself and the inability to look past them
3. Are often a way we grapple with the unknown, and try to make sense of it

A powerful group of autonomous mages and scientists have entered into collusion to strip the land around a city of all its resources, selling them off to traders from Alipinn and Risbullah. This has left the land desolate, the air choked with the smoke of fires and a cloying miasma of corpses of whip lizards and elds who have been killed for their valuable hides and furs but whose meat is too bountiful for the residents of the city.
 
If confronted, they will claim to be doing only what's right for their people. They want, more than anything, to do what's best for as many of their people as possible. They point to the facts ￼

1. They equally distribute all funds to everyone in their city.
2. They do not harm creatures they consider sentient.
3. Their people were previously dedicated stewards of the land, and often eschewed material things to do what was best for the land.
4. They did this out of the belief that they were indebted to their predecessors, as they were also given a land carefully stewarded.
5. There was a cultural sea-change when a great thinker brought about the problem of non-identity.
    1. Circumstances of the world are self-contained, e.g. a thing only happens or has not happened. Though things may be causal, in that every occurrence has a cause, hypothetical events are meaningless as they lack a causal connection.
        1. e.g. , A hypothetical scenario where you lived a different life can never be true because you would no longer be the person having that thought. There would be no causal link that caused you to be the person having that thought in this other world; ergo, that person does not and cannot exist.
    2. If your parents had altered even one thing about how you were conceived, there is an infinitesimally small chance that you would exist.
        1. For these people, the exact conditions of how they are formed causes them to become who they are. Variations in air temperature, humidity, even the relative position of the great squid of the eastern seas can affect the final product.
    3. Therefore, holding the prior two propositions, no matter what one does, only those things that did happen could have happened in order for a living character to be present.
    4. Therefore, no matter the morality of the prior actions, it is necessary for those actions to have taken place for the life experiencing them to exist.
    5. Given the above, we should consider only the actual, present lives when we assess how best to manage affairs, and ignore those hypothetical lives that may or may not happen as they cannot happen if there is no causal link and therefore should not be mourned or lamented.
    6. Ergo, it is best to maximize the happiness and ease of comfort for those currently alive, accepting that these are the only true lives. And in doing so, rest assured that the subsequent lives could not have happened were it not for that self-same consumption.
