2. Greater good?
 
The Declaimer and the Order of the Sixth Form
 
Ginny Steddenward once lived a happy life. Her and her partner Thera lived on the shores the smallest of the three sisters Maron, the lakes surrounding the city-state of [Medilaac](The%20Land%20of%20Tidwell/World%20Details/Nations%20&%20States/Carnithia/Medilaac/Medilaac.md). There, they operated a kelp farm with the help of their two spawn Ark and Oronny. They were always relatively happy, but happiest during the time of the Carnival of the Low Sun.

Traveling merchants, playwrights and their productions, and various amusements from lands as far as The Waste would fill the communities around Medilaac at the time of the spawn. 

Though Isdaans like Ginny lived long lives, they were generally only fertile for two to three seasons, and the Carnival of the Low Sun is therefore important not only as a cultural time of unity, but also to ensure the continuation of their people. Isdaans coming of age would use this time to find partners. Though the period of procreation is brief, the bonds formed during that time are intense and lead to a lasting relationship which holds a pair together for their entire lives.

Ark contracted a disease that caused his gills to become brittle, putting his life in grave danger. He would choke in the water. Staying out of the water caused him to dehydrate. Keeping him well became a full time job for Thera.

Ginny sought medicines for Ark, but his disease persisted. She did everything she could, but finally came to accept that he would die. This pained her greatly.

Thera couldn't accept it, however. He disappeared in the evening with a cart pulled by a [Deaconfish](Deaconfish.md). He traveled south, to the [Gyhrra Badlands](Gyhrra%20Badlands.md), in search of the [Wishing Turtle](Wishing%20Turtle.md). Ginny watched Ark die while he was gone. By the time he returned, Oronny had contracted the same disease. Thera insisted he could fix it with his gift from the Wishing Turtle. With some elaborate setup, he brought Oronny to the water and saying words over her. 
Whatever Thera had done, it worked. Oronny was healed. Thera didn't disclose his wish, and both he and Ginny attempted to return to a normal life. However, word got out about Oronny's miraculous recovery.
 
- People began coming to plead with Thera to heal wounds. Small things, large things. Thera refused, repeatedly.
- Eventually, another child fell ill, with the same disease that claimed Ark.
- Thera healed the child, and after that, the floodgates were open.
- Over the course of ten years, Thera healed a total of 2,417 life-threatening illnesses and injuries, mostly children, though he was pressured into healing the Mayor-Captain of Medilaac and two tribal warlords who had threatened the city in lieu of raids
 
What Thera didn't disclose is that the healing came at a cost. Every life saved cost 10 random lives in response. He was responsible for 24,170 deaths in total. He assured Ginny that every death was sure to be someone that they did not know, and whose lives would never cross their paths. ￼￼The confession came in the afterglow of another carnival of the Low Sun. Thera, inebriated and feeling guilty, told Ginny everything.
 
Ginny killed Thera in response. Over the course of six days, she lived with his corpse, dissociating further. During this dissociation, she made the decision that she would right these wrongs by balancing the ledger, and remove those saved by her partner's curse. 

She started with Oronny.

For the next decade, Ginny lived the life of a shadow, picking off next the Mayor-Captain of Medilaac, and the two tribal warlords before working her way down the line of now young adults. 

Every death was done quietly. Ginny preferred poisons, being impersonal and clean. She made her way through 58 of these. In the process of stalking her 59th victim, she witnessed the young Spittletusk healing their own injured child, and it occurred to her that the curse was communicable to those who it had healed.

Ginny was dreaming too small. The only way to right things was to cleanse the world, and restart the age. 

So she transitioned her plans.

She used the stories that had sprung up around her as a vengeful spirit, attracting disciples that would hear her reasons. At first, these were mostly assassins and others plied in violent trades. But her appeal spread, as she offered absolution for a world overrun with curses. Perhaps more importantly, she hastened a chance for Tidwell to start fresh.
