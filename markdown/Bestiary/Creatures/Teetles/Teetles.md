Teetles are large, thick carapaced beetles with a ridge of fur about their heads and legs. Two comically large eyes dot the front of their faces, and a loose thin pink proboscis hangs between them. They are surface feeders, and while moving, Teetles extend this proboscis to scrape and suck on the bluish-grey stone underfoot. They are consuming the algal and microscopic growth upon these surfaces constantly while moving.
