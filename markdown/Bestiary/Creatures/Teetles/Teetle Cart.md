A common means of conveyance in [Carnithia 1](Carnithia%201.md). Typically a long, low cart with large wheels and poles extending from an oversized tongue on the front of the cart, upon which two or four [Teetles](The%20Land%20of%20Tidwell/Bestiary/Creatures/Teetles/Teetles.md) are yoked. These [Teetles](The%20Land%20of%20Tidwell/Bestiary/Creatures/Teetles/Teetles.md) are guided by long thin strands of rope tied in a loop about their proboscises. When pulled upon, the proboscis is closed and pulled back, and the Teetle comes to a stop as it can no longer eat. When eased off, the proboscis opens and swings down, and the Teetle is compelled to move to eat. In order to turn, a delicate pattern of tugs will impede the eating of Teetles to one side of the cart.
