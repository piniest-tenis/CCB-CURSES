**Tier 1 Standard**
_A semi-aquatic, lunged beast of burden corrupted by the Creep. Where healthy Deaconfish are docile and nimble despite their bulk, these specimens have grown erratic — their numerous muscled pseudopods twitch independently, their large humped backs are split with dark growths, and they lash out at anything that enters the water near them. Some still bear the remnants of harnesses from when they served the people of Gatehouse._
**Motives & Tactics**: Panic, lash out, flee to deeper water
***
Difficulty: 11
Thresholds: 5/9
HP: 4
Stress: 2
Attack: +1
Weapon: Pseudopod Lash: Close | 1d6+2 Physical
***
**Features**
**Flailing Pseudopods - Passive**: Any creature that enters Melee range of the Creep-Touched Deaconfish for the first time on their turn takes 1d4 physical damage from its uncontrolled thrashing.
**Panicked Dive - Reaction**: When the Deaconfish takes damage that marks its Major threshold, it dives beneath the surface and moves up to Far range. It resurfaces at the start of its next spotlight.
**Remnant Harness**: A PC who succeeds on an Instinct Roll (12) notices the old harness. A subsequent Finesse Roll (13) allows the PC to seize the harness and calm the creature, removing it from combat. A calmed Deaconfish can be used as a mount for the remainder of the scene.
