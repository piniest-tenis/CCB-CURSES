**Tier 1 Solo**
_A salamander-like creature of immense proportion, nearly forty yards long, its cavernous mouth wide enough to swallow a Teetle Cart whole. Decades or centuries of exposure to the Creep have left its skin mottled with patches of dark, fibrous growth and its eyes clouded with a milky iridescence. It bears intelligence — it attacked Gatehouse once, timing its assault for when the garrison was depleted. That attack collapsed the northernmost tower of Eskikale Keep._
**Motives & Tactics**: Territorial dominance, patient ambush, destroy threats to its domain
***

**Features**
**Submerged Approach - Passive**: The Skelkandi begins any encounter submerged. It cannot be targeted by attacks until it surfaces. It surfaces when it attacks or when a PC succeeds on an Instinct Roll (14) to spot the disturbance in the water.
**Capsize**: On a successful Lunge attack against a target on a watercraft, the Skelkandi can attempt to capsize the vessel. All passengers must make an Agility Reaction Roll (12) or be thrown into the water, becoming Vulnerable until their next turn.
**Drag Under**: Spend a Fear. The Skelkandi seizes a target within Melee range and pulls them beneath the surface. The target must make a Strength Roll (13) at the start of each of their turns or take 1d6+2 physical damage and remain submerged. Other PCs must enter the water to assist.
**Creep-Scarred Hide - Passive**: The fibrous Creep growth on the Skelkandi's skin hardens against blows. The first time each scene the Skelkandi takes damage that would mark its Severe threshold, reduce the damage by 4.
