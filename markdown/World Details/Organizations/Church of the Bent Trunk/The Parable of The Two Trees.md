At the edge of old Forestdown, in an age before this one, there grew two trees next to one another: A straight, tall tree, proud in its appearance and glorious to behold, and a small bent tree, clinging low to the ground, humble and barren.

Each morning, the Straight Tree would rise with the sun, branches outstretched and leaves upturned, soaking in the whole of the morning light. It would look down at the Bent Tree, and exclaim, "Look at me, tall and straight. My arms reach the sky, and my leaves drink the sun. My boughs spread wide and cover the sky, drinking in the cool air.
"Now, look at you. You are twisted, and low. Your leaves wither and fall when they find no sun, and your bark is rotten and peeling where it touches the ground. What a horrible existence is your life. I pity you."

Every day, this continued. As the Straight Tree grew taller, the Bent Tree struggled more. The Straight tree greedily soaked up all the sun, and its deep roots drank up all the soil. The Straight Tree would look upon the Bent Tree and say, "Why cling to this life, so sad and pitiful? Surely you would be better to give up, let your wood rot and decompose so that it might feed my strong roots and straight trunk. It's selfish, clinging to life as such."

For years, the Straight Tree grew taller, and the Bent Tree barely survived, curled in a twisted mess low to the forest floor. Even when the Straight Tree had grown too tall to comfortably talk to the Bent Tree, it would shout from the canopy, "Today, the sun has blessed me with its radiance, and the soil has gifted me its sustenance. What has it given you, Bent Tree? Nothing, for I am its favored child."

One day, a working gang arrived in the copse where the Straight Tree and the Bent Tree grew. The foreman surveyed the land, and walked through the shade of the tall trees around him. When he was satisfied of the situation, he looked to his crew, and declared loudly, "Crew! Look about you at these fine woods. This may be the most impressive set of tall, plumb trees I have ever seen."

The Straight Tree beamed, spreading its boughs wide, basking in the praise. *Even the lowly creatures that walk beneath my canopy can recognize my magnificence* it thought to itself.

Then, the foreman called loudly, "Cut down all the straight trees. Leave the rest. That will be more than enough."

The Bent Tree grows there, to this day. Low, twisted, alone, and thriving.
