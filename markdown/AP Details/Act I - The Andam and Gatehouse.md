# Act I - The Andam and Gatehouse

## Core Question

The central philosophical question of this story is:

> Is change inherently moral? Can it be good or bad on its surface? And if so, who or what makes that determination?

Act I should not answer this question. It should make the question unavoidable.

Forestdown changes things. The Hygiane Order prevents change. The Varjalune Republic changed the Andam from sacred repayment into negotiated underpayment. Grandmarshal Pogg has been changed by the Creep and hidden by people who prefer institutional continuity to truth. The Spirit Ichida is change itself, acting as if alteration is not only necessary but merciful.

None of these positions should be treated as obviously correct.

---

## Campaign Premise

It is **Eraldama** in the Varjalune Republic: the dry Kuivatuul winds have begun blowing in from The Waste, opening the trade routes to the interior of Tidwell. In **Alipinn**, linens are shaken out, windows thrown open, Paarlid accounts reconciled, Kybon goods displayed, and every family quietly counts who among them might be called.

This is the season of celebration.

It is also the season of **the Andam**.

The Andam is the yearly tribute of persons dedicated to the Hygiane Order. It was cemented 231 years ago after the **Eraldama Incursion**, when Taliga was nearly consumed by Etherotaxia and Creep spreading out of Forestdown through Erelbaad Lake. The Hygiane Order's Medicaments and Hospitallers saved Taliga, though a fifth of the population did not survive intact.

At its height, the Andam sent as many as 700 young Varjalune west to Gatehouse each year. Now it sends 150, drawn from volunteers and those compelled by force. The Varjalune Republic has negotiated the obligation downward for generations, substituting Paarlid and tribute goods where it once sent children. The Hygiane Order once tried to enforce the Andam by marching on Alipinn, but had to turn back when Gatehouse nearly fell to the Skelkandi.

Now the Hygiane Order arrives in Varjalune to collect the 231st Andam.

They arrive looking proud enough, but not strong enough.

Reveille is distant in Act I: a city heard about in rumors of the Sixth Form, the Renewal ceremony, disrupted politics, and imperial distraction. The PCs do not visit it.

# Session 1: The Form That Thanks You for Your Life

## Session Premise

The PCs are in the Varjalune Republic, either in Alipinn or newly arrived there from Taliga and surrounding communities, when the Andam names are posted and collected. They are processed by a small Hygiane delegation with ceremonial authority but little real local power, then sent west toward Gatehouse.

Session 1 should establish:

- The Andam is both sacred debt and resented extraction.
- The Hygiane Order is known through old epics, not current competence.
- Varjalune prosperity rests partly on a reduced obligation to the people still holding Forestdown back.
- Grandmarshal Pogg's office is already manipulating the draft.
- Forestdown's new activity can be felt even before the characters reach Gatehouse.

---

## Phase 1: The Draft in Alipinn

### Location: The Alipinn Eraldama Exchange Hall

Begin in **Alipinn**, the major trade town at the mouth of **Vaiknelaht Bay**. Eraldama has turned the city outward. Damp linens hang from windows. Dry Kuivatuul wind snaps flags from merchant balconies. Kybon goods sit behind guarded shutters. Paarlid ledgers are being audited in public, because in Alipinn money becomes more trustworthy when embarrassed in front of neighbors.

The draft is conducted in the **Eraldama Exchange Hall**, a civic building normally used for opening-season trade compacts. Today, one half remains a market tribunal; the other has been curtained off with faded Hygiane banners and a Varjalune civic notice reading:

> THE 231ST ANDAM WILL PROCEED WITH ALL APPROPRIATE GRATITUDE.

Below it, someone has written:

> GRATITUDE IS EASIER FOR THOSE WHO STAY.

### Why Alipinn

Alipinn is the political and commercial center of the Republic. The draft happens here because this is where tribute is counted, negotiated, converted, and disguised as civic virtue. Taliga's history is invoked constantly, but Alipinn's clerks decide what the debt means this year.

If the party is more Taliga-centered, open with them arriving from Taliga by lake-road or trade cart that morning, passing memorial markers from the Eraldama Incursion.

### Sensory Texture

- Dry wind moving through a city built for wet seasons.
- Salt crust on window hinges and account-books.
- Paarlid merchants arguing about valuation while trying not to look at the draft list.
- Ayonik and Allonaut visitors from Taliga quietly touching old memorial beads.
- Hygiane banners patched so many times the original color has become a rumor.
- A table of old Hospitaller epics for children, sold beside cheap mourning ribbons.

### The Hygiane Delegation

The Hygiane delegation has no sustained presence in Varjalune. They arrive to collect the Andam and tribute, relying on old authority, local guilt, and legal custom. They are outnumbered by Varjalune civic officers and watched by a crowd that is half grateful, half resentful, and half trying to calculate whether 150 is negotiable.

Yes, that is three halves. It is Alipinn. The accountants permit it during Eraldama.

### Key NPC: Captain-Bursar Olt Venn

- **Species:** Riolan
- **Affiliation:** Andam Office / Hygiane Order collection delegation
- **Appearance:** Faded ochre skin, a ledger-harness across his belly, three quills tucked into the soft folds behind his jaw. His back bristles rattle softly when he lies, which is often but politely.
- **Demeanor:** Courteous, exhausted, institutionally sincere.
- **Motivation:** Deliver 150 bodies and the negotiated tribute west to Gatehouse.
- **Secret:** This year's lists include names added directly by Grandmarshal Pogg's office. Several added names belong to people with documented curses, family curse histories, or prior exposure to Forestdown phenomena.

Useful line:

> Please understand: the Republic has already expressed appreciation for your service in aggregate. I am here to apply that appreciation to individuals.

### Varjalune Civic Counterpart: Assessor Maali Vesk

- **Species:** Ayonik
- **Affiliation:** Alipinn civic assessor, Andam liaison
- **Appearance:** Heavy oiled coat despite the dry season, large glassy eyes, ink-stained claws.
- **Demeanor:** Loquacious, neighborly, relentlessly evasive.
- **Motivation:** Keep Alipinn's obligations minimized without looking dishonorable.
- **Secret:** She knows some names were substituted onto the Andam list in exchange for reduced Paarlid tribute. She does not know why the Hygiane wanted those particular people.

Useful line:

> We honor Taliga by remembering the debt. We honor Alipinn by interpreting the debt with precision.

### Other Newly Drafted Recruits

#### Sella Brinehook

- **Species:** Isdaan
- **Affiliation:** Taliga lake-family, drafted after her family failed to meet an adjusted tribute assessment.
- **Demeanor:** Sardonic, elegant, angry beneath composure.
- **Secret:** Her family has received letters from the Order of the Sixth Form promising that the Renewal will make all debts and curses irrelevant.
- **Curse Detail:** A pearl is lodged under her left gill. Whenever she lies, she tastes lakewater from the night Taliga burned green in family stories.

#### Moddick "Mod" Harrow

- **Species:** Human
- **Affiliation:** Alipinn dock-worker attached to Kybon cargo crews.
- **Demeanor:** Cheerfully fatalistic. Calls everyone "supervisor."
- **Secret:** He volunteered before the draft could take his younger sister.
- **Curse Detail:** Owns a cursed thimble inherited from his mother. A thimbleful of any liquid he drinks becomes black pitch in his stomach. He eats constantly and drinks only by ritual measurement.

#### Pebble-Under-Red-Moss

- **Species:** Goran
- **Affiliation:** Caravan guard stranded in Alipinn for Eraldama trade.
- **Demeanor:** Patient, literal, dryly funny.
- **Secret:** Believes the Tree of Life is not sacred but invasive.
- **Curse Detail:** A small brass bell is embedded in his shoulder-stone. It rings whenever someone nearby makes a promise they do not intend to keep.

#### Sister Caul

- **Species:** Hokaw
- **Affiliation:** Davite lay attendant traveling with Taliga memorial pilgrims.
- **Demeanor:** Warm, severe, prayerful.
- **Secret:** She believes the Creep may be part of the Revenant God's dream, and containment may be mercy or blasphemy. She has not decided which.
- **Curse Detail:** Her blind left eye sees only places where someone has died afraid.

### The Draft Procedure

Each PC is processed through five stations:

1. **Name Posting**
   Their name appears on a public slate. The slate is watered with Erelbaad Lake water and dried by Kuivatuul wind to prove the obligation survives both wet and dry seasons.

2. **Civic Objection Window**
   Family, employers, debtors, rivals, and strangers may object to the draft. Most objections are rejected by formula. One merchant objects to losing a dockhand and is told, "Your grief has been received at commercial weight."

3. **Curse and Exposure Declaration**
   The PCs are asked to declare cursed trinkets, inherited curse histories, prior Forestdown exposure, or dreams involving trees, doors, books, lakewater, windows, invisible streets, or benevolent ghosts.

4. **Tribute Conversion Audit**
   A clerk determines whether anyone could be replaced by Paarlid, goods, or civic credit. This year, the answer is almost always no. The delegation's private instructions require bodies, not substitutes.

5. **Oath of Temporary Continuance**
   The Hygiane officer reads a vow. The PCs may repeat it, refuse, mock it, or alter it. The vow ends: **I agree to stand between Tidwell and what Tidwell has already done to itself.**

### Institutional Absurdity

Each recruit receives a **Welcome Packet for Involuntary Excellence**, revised for Varjalune service:

- One bootlace, for morale.
- A map of Gatehouse printed upside down because Forestdown is better approached from humility.
- A civic receipt proving the Republic has valued their departure.
- A folded card reading: **If you experience spontaneous rooting, notify a Tender before notifying loved ones.**
- A reimbursement form for funeral expenses, requiring the deceased's signature and a witness from their original municipality.

### GM Flag: Slow Down

Ask each player:

> What part of your character's life did the Andam interrupt, and who in the crowd fails to meet their eyes?

This is the first moral change of the campaign: the characters' lives are altered by an old debt they did not personally incur.

### Curse Complication: The Declaration Basket

During processing, the curse-declaration basket coughs up a small object no one put there: a **bone button threaded with green hair**.

It rolls toward one PC. If touched, it gives a brief vision:

- Taliga's lake surface turning still as glass.
- A ruined northern tower.
- A one-legged Ghotling sewing buttons onto his uniform.
- Grandmarshal Pogg coughing up a fern.
- A voice, joyful and old, saying: **Change is not coming. Change has been waiting.**

The button then becomes inert. No one in the hall admits seeing it.

### GM Flag: Lean In

Play the clerks and assessors as compassionate but trapped inside arithmetic. They are not cruel. They are people who have mistaken accurate accounting for moral clarity.

## Phase 2: The Journey West

### Route

The PCs travel from Alipinn toward Gatehouse with the 231st Andam. The route begins in Eraldama commerce: wagons of Kybon goods, Paarlid security escorts, dry wind, loud road contracts, and celebratory departures that become quieter the farther west the convoy goes.

If starting in Taliga, the first leg follows Erelbaad Lake, where memorial posts still mark the thirty days of the Eraldama Incursion. Ayonik elders and Allonaut partners watch the convoy pass with the particular sadness of people whose grandparents survived because someone else's children were sent away.

The convoy includes:

- Two draft wagons.
- One tribute wagon with sealed Paarlid accounts and replacement goods.
- A small Hygiane collection delegation.
- Six returning Andam veterans.
- A caged lantern filled with pale moths that all face west.
- One Varjalune civic observer, officially present to ensure good treatment and unofficially present to ensure the Republic gets a receipt.

### Landscape Progression

As the convoy moves away from Varjalune:

- Dry Kuivatuul wind weakens, then stops.
- Trade-road certainties give way to borderland repairs.
- Moss appears on the wrong side of stones.
- Birds begin repeating fragments of old Hospitaller epics incorrectly.
- Road signs become more helpful and less accurate.
- Tree roots stop crossing the road and begin measuring it.

### Veteran Recruit: Hask Bellweather

- **Species:** Human
- **Affiliation:** Hospitaller auxiliary, third Andam term
- **Demeanor:** Funny, tired, kind in a way that has been sanded down.
- **Secret:** He saw Pogg last month and knows something is terribly wrong, but has convinced himself he must have misunderstood.

Useful line:

> The Hygiane in the songs were giants. The Hygiane at Gatehouse are people with wet socks and old knives. Try not to hold that against them. Giants are expensive.

Hask explains the four wings:

- **Hospitallers:** knights, clerics, and Weird trained against major haunts, corrupted creatures, and abominations.
- **Tenders:** perimeter walkers who salt the underbrush and call for Medicaments when a burn is needed.
- **Medicaments:** clerics, scientists, and researchers who study Forestdown's effects and treat affected populations.
- **Etherements:** mediums, Weird, and the cursed who study spirits and haunts, especially Ichida.

He warns the PCs:

> Don't get assigned to the northern tower unless you have a very settled opinion on whether your dreams are legally yours.

### Road Encounter: The Orchard That Interprets the Debt

The convoy passes a dry orchard planted decades earlier by Varjalune families as a memorial to those sent in the Andam. Each tree is tagged with a year and a number.

The trees have bent into arches, forming a corridor pointing west.

The fruit hanging from the branches are not fruit but tiny sealed paper packets. Each packet contains a name.

Some names belong to people in the convoy. Some belong to people not yet born. One packet bears **Grandmarshal Pogg** written backward. Another bears **Taliga**, as if the town itself can be drafted.

### Situation

A Tender patrol is already present, arguing with a Medicament field researcher.

#### Tender Patrol Leader: Yarra Vost

- **Species:** Spittletusk
- **Affiliation:** Tenders
- **Demeanor:** Practical, impatient, mud to the knees.
- **Secret:** She has been ordered to underreport Creep activity along this stretch.

#### Medicament Researcher: Pell Oom

- **Species:** Ghotling
- **Affiliation:** Medicaments
- **Demeanor:** Bright-eyed, twitchy, carrying too many sample jars.
- **Secret:** Has been cataloging Lanmaoa exposure and is less sober than he sounds.

### Player Choices

- Help burn the orchard immediately.
- Preserve samples for study.
- Take one or more name-packets.
- Question why this was not in the road warnings.
- Ask whether this change is corruption, communication, or remembrance.

### Consequences

- **Burning it:** earns Tender respect, loses Medicament data, and enrages some Varjalune observers who view the orchard as memorial ground.
- **Studying it:** delays the convoy; something in the orchard learns a PC's voice.
- **Taking a packet:** creates a future curse hook. The bearer dreams of the named person standing in a door made of leaves.
- **Questioning orders:** reveals the first hint that reports are being altered before reaching command.
- **Naming it as remembrance:** causes the orchard to briefly become less hostile, but one PC later hears a voice ask, "If change preserves grief, why do you call it rot?"

### GM Flag: Slow Down

The orchard is not attacking. It is changing a memorial into an argument. Let the players sit with whether that makes it evil.

## Phase 3: Arrival at Gatehouse

### First View

Gatehouse is a bustling border town on the edge of **Ergantine Lake**, home to the Hygiane Order's massive fortress and monastery on an easily defensible outcropping into the lake.

It should not look empty. It should look strained.

The town still works: fishers, laborers, orderlies, innkeepers, families of long-term soldiers, salt merchants, exhausted clerics, and civilian repair crews. But the institution at its center is visibly less than the epics promised.

### Eskikale Keep

The fortress-monastery is granite and living Sallidean carvings, ancient and patched by centuries of anxious additions. It has three functioning wings and one destroyed northern tower. The whole place looks like a monastery that survived a war by becoming a warehouse and then regretted it.

### What the Andam Songs Promised

- Proud banners.
- Unified command.
- Ancient discipline.
- Hospitallers like giants.
- A bastion against corruption.

### What the PCs Actually See

- Banners faded to the color of old teeth.
- A gate winch operated by three arguing clerks and a mule.
- Hospitaller veterans drilling with broom handles because the polearms were temporarily reallocated.
- Tenders sleeping in shifts on sacks of salt.
- Medicaments hauling sample jars past the mess hall.
- Etherements watching from the broken northern tower, each wearing too many buttons.

The welcome sign reads:

> GATEHOUSE: HOLDING FIRM SINCE THE FOURTH ADMINISTRATIVE REVISION

Someone has crossed out **firm** and written **mostly**.

### Arrival NPCs

#### Rearcaptain Grace Kinder

- **Species:** Human
- **Faction:** Tenders
- **Appearance:** Short blonde hair, stern face, dress uniform worn constantly to cover the deep scar on her right arm.
- **Demeanor:** Disciplined, exhausted, quietly furious.
- **Motivation:** Keep the perimeter salted and her people alive.
- **Position:** Suspicious of command, but loyal to the mission.
- **Secret:** She has received contradictory orders in Pogg's name, including one that would have left an entire border stretch unsalted. She disobeyed and falsified compliance.

First impression: Grace personally inspects the recruits because no one else did it correctly. She apologizes for the state of the boots they are issued, then threatens to feed the quartermaster his own inventory ledger if any recruit is sent to the perimeter barefoot.

#### Rearcaptain Oramora

- **Species:** Ghotling
- **Faction:** Etherements
- **Appearance:** Missing right leg, no prosthesis, moves in a swinging arm-lope. Uniform covered in excess buttons.
- **Demeanor:** Brooding, evasive, intense.
- **Motivation:** Understand Ichida before Ichida finishes whatever it has begun.
- **Position:** Believes the Order is already obsolete; wants access, not discipline.
- **Secret:** One of his expelled Etherements walked willingly into Forestdown and recently sent back a letter written on living bark. Oramora has hidden it.

First impression: He watches the recruits arrive from a high walkway. When asked if he needs assistance descending, he says, "No. Down is where everyone is already going."

#### Quarter-Adjunct Millow Fleen

- **Species:** Human
- **Faction:** Pogg's Inner Staff / corrupt toadies
- **Appearance:** Soft hands, perfumed collar, ceremonial sword too polished to have opinions.
- **Demeanor:** Smiling, obstructionist, weaponized politeness.
- **Motivation:** Keep the Grandmarshal unseen; continue selling Order assets.
- **Position:** Publicly loyal to Pogg. Privately looting the institution.
- **Secret:** He has sold Varjalune tribute chests and Paarlid replacement accounts to private collectors and replaced them with stones wrapped in inventory cloth.

First impression: Fleen gives the official welcome in front of a covered statue no one can identify. He repeatedly refers to **the Grandmarshal's robust confidence** and **recent strategic quietude**.

#### Brother-Mender Ossa Vey

- **Species:** Spittletusk
- **Faction:** Medicaments
- **Appearance:** Long lower tusks capped in cracked copper; apron stained with reagents and soup.
- **Demeanor:** Maternal, distracted, far too calm about horror.
- **Motivation:** Treat infection, preserve knowledge, keep the Medicaments from collapsing into indulgent research habits.
- **Position:** Knows something is wrong with Pogg but lacks access.
- **Secret:** Has seen Creep samples grow into geometric patterns matching old maps of the Invisible City.

### First Hint of the Pogg Situation

During the arrival address, Fleen announces:

> Grandmarshal Pogg regrets that he cannot greet the 231st Andam personally, owing to his current state of victorious contemplation.

From somewhere inside the keep comes a wet, antlered coughing sound. Everyone pretends not to hear it.

Grace Kinder's face tightens. Oramora smiles without amusement. A junior clerk begins applauding too early, panics, and turns it into a salute.

### First Hint of Strange Creep Behavior

A Tender runner arrives with a sealed perimeter report. Fleen tries to intercept it. If the PCs notice or intervene, they glimpse the report:

- Three salt lines breached.
- No random spread.
- Creep growth forming repeated archways.
- Infected moss orienting toward Gatehouse like iron filings toward a magnet.
- A phrase scratched into a dead tree by no visible hand: **NOT DEATH. REVISION.**

### End of Session Image

That night, the recruits are assigned bunks in a damp barracks where the walls breathe faintly because the Sallidean Stone has not been properly soothed.

Across Ergantine Lake, deep in Forestdown, a line of pale lights rises one by one among the trees.

Not like campfires.

Like windows.

Then the lights rearrange themselves into the shape of an open book.

# Act I Story Beats

Act I should feel like a garden of pressures. The sessions below are not a railroad; they are escalating situations. Let the players choose whom to trust, which evidence to pursue, and what kind of Order they want to help build from the ruins of the old one.

## Session 1: The Form That Thanks You for Your Life

- **Primary Situation:** The Andam draft in Alipinn during Eraldama, the road west, arrival at Gatehouse.
- **A Plot:** The memorial orchard shows the Creep forming deliberate structures and philosophical arguments.
- **B Plot:** Pogg is absent; Fleen gives evasive explanations; a strange cough is heard.
- **Key NPCs:** Captain-Bursar Olt Venn, Assessor Maali Vesk, Grace Kinder, Oramora, Millow Fleen.
- **Player Choice:** How do they respond to the orchard: burn, study, preserve, interpret, or ignore?
- **Closing Image:** Forest lights rearrange into an open book.

## Session 2: Orientation Beneath the Damp Bell

- **Primary Situation:** Recruits are assigned temporary duties among the four wings.
- **A Plot:** Tenders report salt lines failing in non-natural patterns. The Creep avoids some old boundary stones while overrunning newer defenses.
- **B Plot:** A new edict in Pogg's name reassigns salt reserves away from Grace Kinder's patrols to ceremonial readiness.
- **Key NPCs:** Grace Kinder, Brother-Mender Ossa Vey, Hask Bellweather, Quarter-Adjunct Fleen.
- **Player Choice:** Which wing do the PCs assist first?
- **Closing Image:** The keep's damp bell rings without being struck; every cursed object in the barracks turns west-northwest toward Forestdown.

## Session 3: Inventory of Missing Teeth

- **Primary Situation:** The PCs are drawn into a supply audit after a patrol lacks proper salt, weapons, and burn permits.
- **A Plot:** Without supplies, a small Creep bloom reaches a lakeside hamlet near Gatehouse.
- **B Plot:** Evidence emerges that Varjalune tribute chests and Paarlid accounts have been emptied or redirected. Fleen blames Andam wastage and Varjalune underpayment.
- **Key NPCs:** Millow Fleen, Mod Harrow, Yarra Vost, a nervous clerk named Tembit Sore.
- **Player Choice:** Expose the missing supplies publicly or quietly gather more evidence?
- **Closing Image:** A tribute chest is opened. Inside: stones, dead moths, and one fresh green leaf.

## Session 4: The Medicaments' Kindly Errors

- **Primary Situation:** The PCs assist the Medicaments with infected civilians and unstable researchers.
- **A Plot:** Creep victims speak in architectural metaphors: "The door is learning us." "The city has no outside anymore."
- **B Plot:** Ossa Vey reveals he has been denied access to Pogg despite medical necessity.
- **Key NPCs:** Brother-Mender Ossa Vey, Pell Oom, Sister Caul, infected civilian Marrit.
- **Player Choice:** Do the PCs prioritize treatment, research, or containment?
- **Closing Image:** A child's infection recedes, leaving under the skin a map of streets no one can see unless they close one eye.

## Session 5: The Northern Tower Dreams Back

- **Primary Situation:** The PCs enter the ruined northern tower where the Etherements reside.
- **A Plot:** Oramora shows them evidence: Creep growths correspond to rumored districts of the Invisible City.
- **B Plot:** Oramora claims Pogg's orders have not sounded like Pogg for months.
- **Key NPCs:** Rearcaptain Oramora, the expelled Etherement's bark-letter, Etherement acolytes.
- **Player Choice:** Share Oramora's information with the other wings, keep it secret, or use it to bargain?
- **Closing Image:** A button falls from Oramora's coat and rolls uphill toward Forestdown.

## Session 6: Salt Line, Softly Singing

- **Primary Situation:** A perimeter patrol with the Tenders.
- **A Plot:** The Creep does not attack. It constructs. It grows root-bridges over salt lines and leaves living gates in the underbrush.
- **B Plot:** Grace admits she falsified compliance with a suicidal order issued in Pogg's name.
- **Key NPCs:** Grace Kinder, Yarra Vost, Pebble-Under-Red-Moss, Tender patrol.
- **Player Choice:** Obey the official map or follow the Creep's new architecture?
- **Closing Image:** The PCs find a clearing where dozens of discarded curse-trinkets hang from branches like offerings.

## Session 7: The Tribunal of Small Receipts

- **Primary Situation:** Fleen and the inner staff retaliate, accusing the PCs and allied officers of disorder, theft, or spiritual contamination.
- **A Plot:** During the tribunal, Forestdown sends a haunt through the paperwork itself: ink turns into rootlets, testimony rearranges.
- **B Plot:** The toadies reveal themselves through overreach. Their forged orders do not match Pogg's old command style.
- **Key NPCs:** Millow Fleen, two additional toadies, Grace Kinder, Ossa Vey, Oramora.
- **Player Choice:** Fight procedurally, stage a counter-investigation, appeal to wing loyalties, or break into command records.
- **Closing Image:** A forged order bleeds green sap from Pogg's signature.

## Session 8: Audience with the Grandmarshal

- **Primary Situation:** The PCs gain access to Pogg.
- **A Plot:** Pogg's infection reacts to news of the strange Creep patterns; for a moment he speaks with clarity and terror.
- **B Plot:** The truth: Pogg is profoundly infected, lucid only in flashes, and his staff has been ruling through him.
- **Key NPCs:** Grandmarshal Pogg, Fleen, Ossa Vey, Grace Kinder.
- **Player Choice:** What is Pogg owed: public exposure, private removal, medical containment, mercy killing, or a final chance to command?
- **Closing Image:** Pogg, enormous and ruined, asks: "Did I hold long enough? Please tell me I held long enough."

**Expected B Plot Resolution Begins Here.** This is where the players should decide what justice and mercy look like when change has already happened and cannot be undone.

## Session 9: What Replaces a Spine

- **Primary Situation:** The Order must choose interim leadership or collapse into factional paralysis.
- **A Plot:** Forestdown's movements accelerate. The Creep begins forming causeways toward Gatehouse.
- **B Plot:** The corrupt toadies are exposed, flee, bargain, or attempt sabotage.
- **Key NPCs:** Grace Kinder, Oramora, Ossa Vey, Hospitaller veterans, captured toadies.
- **Player Choice:** Who do the PCs back: Grace Kinder, a Council of Wings, Oramora, Ossa Vey, or a player-shaped coalition?
- **Closing Image:** Pogg's portrait is taken down. Behind it, the wall has grown a living ear.

**B Plot Resolution Lands Here.** Pogg is removed, contained, honored, or killed. The toadies are exposed or displaced. The old command structure is finished.

## Session 10: The Lake Opens Its Eyes

- **Primary Situation:** The first major incursion begins.
- **A Plot:** The Creep advances in organized waves. Ergantine Lake's vast creatures rise, infected not as beasts but as vessels. Forestdown's edge becomes citylike: arches, streets, thresholds.
- **B Plot:** The coalition formed by the PCs is tested immediately.
- **Key NPCs:** All faction representatives; surviving recruits; refugees from nearby hamlets.
- **Player Choice:** What does the new coalition defend first: Gatehouse fortress, civilian town, salt stores, Medicament archives, Northern Tower knowledge, or escape routes?
- **Closing Image:** Across the lake, where trees should be, the Invisible City becomes visible for one impossible minute. It is beautiful. It is wrong. It is waiting.

**A Plot Tips into Act II Here.** Act II begins with the incursion at full scale and the players' Act I alliances becoming the backbone of resistance.

# Faction Map

## The Varjalune Civic Assessors

- **Description:** Alipinn officials who manage tribute, exemptions, substitution, Paarlid accounting, and the civic machinery of the Andam.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Initially unaware of Pogg's condition; highly sensitive to proof that Varjalune tribute has been stolen or misused.
- **Position on Forestdown:** Publicly grateful to the Hygiane Order; privately complacent after two quiet centuries.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Receipts, proof, and assurance that Varjalune is not being exploited beyond the negotiated debt.
- **What They Offer:** Political pressure, supply records, leverage over tribute, witnesses from Alipinn and Taliga.

## Taliga Memorial Families

- **Description:** Families and civic groups whose identity is shaped by the Eraldama Incursion and the survival of Taliga.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Outraged if the Order's decline dishonors the sacrifice that created the Andam.
- **Position on Forestdown:** They remember that Forestdown can reach them. They take the threat more seriously than Alipinn's merchants do.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Protection from another Taliga, respect for the dead, and truth about whether the old bargain still means anything.
- **What They Offer:** Local legitimacy, historical memory, lake-route contacts, Ayonik and Allonaut support.

## The Tenders

- **Description:** Perimeter walkers, salters, burners, and practical containment workers. Least glamorous, most necessary.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Grace Kinder suspects command rot and has already disobeyed dangerous orders.
- **Position on Forestdown:** Contain it. Salt it. Burn when needed. No romance, no theories until the line holds.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Proof, help, and people willing to do ugly work without applause.
- **What They Offer:** Patrol access, local trust, perimeter knowledge, disciplined field teams.

## The Hospitaller Veterans

- **Description:** Old knights, clerics, Weird, and fighters trained against major haunts and abominations. Famous, diminished, half-retired by necessity.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Loyal to the symbol of command; slow to accept betrayal unless shown undeniable proof.
- **Position on Forestdown:** They remember previous incursions and fear this one is worse.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Respect for sacrifice; evidence before accusation.
- **What They Offer:** Combat experience, defensive tactics, morale among common soldiers.

## The Medicaments

- **Description:** Clerics, scientists, and researchers studying Forestdown's effects on bodies, ecology, and communities.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Frustrated by blocked medical access to Pogg; divided between humane practitioners and indulgent researchers.
- **Position on Forestdown:** It must be understood as well as contained.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Samples, protection, ethical cover, and help resisting their own worst impulses.
- **What They Offer:** Treatment, analysis, infection lore, evidence that the Creep is becoming architectural.

## The Etherements

- **Description:** Mediums, Weird, cursed scholars, and haunt-specialists studying spirits and Ichida. Cloistered in the destroyed northern tower.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Believe command is already spiritually compromised, but speak too cryptically to be trusted.
- **Position on Forestdown:** The threat is metaphysical. The Creep is not just spreading; it is communicating change.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Access to field sites, recovered curse-trinkets, protection from purges.
- **What They Offer:** Insight into Ichida, haunt negotiation, dangerous rituals, knowledge of the Invisible City.

## Pogg's Inner Staff

- **Description:** Corrupt advisors and quartermasters propping up the infected Grandmarshal as a figurehead.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Conceal Pogg, rule in his name, sell Order assets and Varjalune tribute.
- **Position on Forestdown:** Publicly alarmed, privately opportunistic. They underestimate the crisis.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Compliance, silence, useful scapegoats.
- **What They Offer as Allies:** Paper authority, forged permits, temporary protection, access to stolen goods.
- **True Use:** Antagonistic pressure for Act I. They are villains because they chose comfort over duty.

## Gatehouse Townsfolk

- **Description:** Civilians, fishers, laborers, innkeepers, families, and workers living under the shadow of Eskikale Keep.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** They know the Order is failing because they live with the consequences.
- **Position on Forestdown:** Terrified, pragmatic, used to being ignored until eaten.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Protection, truth, evacuation plans, someone who does not speak in institutional slogans.
- **What They Offer:** Rumors, local routes, shelter, civilian legitimacy.

## Church of the Revenant God Davites

- **Description:** Faithful in green and white stoles, committed to endurance, mercy, and keeping the Revenant God asleep.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Compassion for Pogg as a sufferer; condemnation of those exploiting him.
- **Position on Forestdown:** Divided. Some see containment as holy mercy; others wonder if change is part of the God's dream.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Mercy in decisions, care for the infected, restraint.
- **What They Offer:** Healers, civilian calm, moral authority, sanctuary.

## Church of the Bent Trunk

- **Description:** A charitable faith for the poor, infirm, disfigured, maladapted, and unwanted.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Instinctively sympathetic to the broken; hostile to institutional cruelty.
- **Position on Forestdown:** Fearful but attentive to those no one else protects, including cursed refugees.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Protection for the discarded and cursed.
- **What They Offer:** Refugee networks, food, rumor channels, moral clarity without political power.

## The Curse-Bearer Drift

- **Description:** Not yet a formal faction. People with powerful curses are being drawn toward Forestdown, some by dreams, some by promises, some by relief.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Mostly indifferent; the Order has rarely helped them.
- **Position on Forestdown:** Some believe Ichida may free them. Some believe Ichida may finally make their suffering meaningful.
- **What They Want from PCs:** To not be treated as contaminants or weapons.
- **What They Offer:** Curse-magic, testimony of Ichida's new activity, a moral test the Order is likely to fail.

## Distant Pressure: The Order of the Sixth Form

- **Description:** The Declaimer's movement, active far away in Reveille and at the Renewal ceremony.
- **Position on Pogg and Corruption:** Not directly involved.
- **Position on Forestdown:** Their ideology indirectly competes with Ichida's: end the age rather than restructure reality.
- **What They Want from PCs:** Nothing directly in Act I.
- **What They Offer:** Background pressure only: disrupted supplies, political distraction, rumors that the world is already ending elsewhere.

# Act I Closing Question

By the end of Act I, the players should not simply ask:

> How do we stop Forestdown?

They should ask:

> If change can heal, destroy, reveal, corrupt, preserve, and liberate, who gets to decide which change is moral?
