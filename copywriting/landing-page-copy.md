## Copy Notes & Rationale

### Voice Decisions

- **"Your Adventure Starts Here"** was chosen over more aggressive options ("Daggerheart Deserves Better Tools") because it's welcoming to all three audiences — players, GMs, and newcomers — without positioning against competitors.
- **"Roll Initiative"** for the final CTA leans into TTRPG language that every player recognizes. It works as both a game term and a call to action.
- **"Curses! This Page Isn't Here Yet."** turns the brand name into the joke naturally — it's what you'd actually say when you hit a 404.

### Terminology Alignment

- All Daggerheart SRD terms use official capitalization: Hope, Stress, HP, Domain, Ancestry, Community, Class, Subclass, Domain Card, Loadout.
- "Adversary" (not "monster" or "enemy") in encounter design copy — per SRD convention.
- "SRD" is used without expansion after first use; the target audience knows what it means.

### Pricing Framing

- The pricing section avoids the word "tier" (which implies hierarchy) in favor of card titles "Player" and "Game Master" — matching the roles players actually identify with.
- "Claim Your Seat" for the GM CTA is more evocative than "Subscribe" or "Get Started" while staying within the fantasy-adjacent voice.
- "$5/month" is stated plainly. No "/mo" abbreviation, no annual upsell — matching the brief's emphasis on honesty and simplicity.

### Accessibility Considerations

- All CTA button text is action-oriented and distinct — no two buttons say the same thing in the same viewport.
- Testimonial attributions include role context (GM, new player, streamer) to help screen reader users understand the perspective.
- The scroll indicator uses the word "Discover" rather than a bare arrow, providing meaning to non-visual users.
- Feature lists use sentence fragments that begin with a verb or noun — scannable for all reading levels.
